  • Development

    Electric vehicle (EV) product development is the process of designing, engineering, and manufacturing electric vehicles that meet consumer needs and preferences while adhering to safety, environmental, and regulatory standards. EV product development requires a deep understanding of EV technology, including battery systems, electric motors, and power electronics. The EV product development process typically involves concept Development, Engineering & Design, Prototyping & Testing, Manufacturing and Market Launch. Our ultimate goal of EV product development is to create high-quality, safe, and reliable electric vehicles that meet the needs and preferences of consumers while contributing to a more sustainable future. EV product development requires a multi-disciplinary team with expertise in engineering, design, manufacturing, marketing, and customer service to ensure a successful launch and ongoing product improvement.

  • Homologation

    Electric vehicle homologation is the process of obtaining the necessary regulatory approvals to sell and operate electric vehicles (EVs) on public roads. Homologation is a legal requirement in our country to ensure that EVs meet the safety and environmental standards set by our government. The homologation process includes various tests, inspections, and certifications that EVs must undergo to meet the regulatory requirements. that are safety, emissions, electromagnetic compatibility, energy consumption and many technical approval. The homologation process is typically carried out by regulatory agencies authorized by our government. Once an EV has successfully undergone homologation, it is granted the necessary approvals to be sold and operated on public roads. The ultimate goal of EV homologation is to ensure that EVs are safe, reliable, and environmentally friendly. Homologation helps to build consumer confidence in EVs and facilitates the widespread adoption of this important technology.

  • Certification

    Electric vehicle (EV) approval certification is a process in which EV manufacturers and suppliers must comply with specific safety and environmental regulations and standards to ensure that their products meet the requirements of the market and the law. The certification process is typically carried out by regulatory agencies. The EV certification process includes various steps, including testing, inspection, and documentation. This process includes testing and inspection documentation, regulatory compliance, type approval and so on. The ultimate goal of EV approval certification is to ensure that EVs are safe, reliable, and environmentally friendly. The certification process helps to build consumer confidence in EVs and facilitates the widespread adoption of this important technology.

  • 3D Modelling

    3D designing of electric vehicles (EVs) is a critical component of the EV product development process. 3D design enables EV manufacturers to create accurate and detailed digital models of the vehicle and its components, which can be used to optimize the design for safety, performance, and efficiency. The 3D design process typically involves concept design, detailed design, simulation and optimization, prototyping and so on. The 3D design process is an essential component of EV product development. 3D design also enables manufacturers to reduce the time and cost associated with traditional physical prototyping, which can speed up the EV product development process.

  • Documentation

    Electric vehicle (EV) approval documentation refers to the set of documents and records required for an EV to obtain regulatory approval for sale and operation on public roads. The approval documentation includes technical specifications, test results, compliance certificates, user manuals, maintenance and repair manuals, production documentation and many more. The approval documentation is a critical component of the EV homologation process. Without the necessary approval documentation, an EV cannot be sold or operated on public roads. The documentation helps to ensure that EVs meet the necessary safety, environmental, and performance standards, which builds consumer confidence in EVs and supports their widespread adoption.

Organisations We Serve
  • ICAT

    International Centre for Automotive Technology (iCAT) is located at Manesar in Gurugram district of Haryana state of India. The Rs 1100 crore facility has facilities for vehicle homologation and also testing laboratories for noise, vibration and harshness (NVH) and passive safety. It also includes a powertrain laboratory, engine dynamometers, emission laboratory with Euro-V capability, a fatigue laboratory, passive safety laboratory, and vehicle test tracks.

  • ARAI

    Automotive Research Association of India (ARAI) is co-operative industrial research association by the automotive industry with the Ministry of Industries, Government Of India[3] The objectives of the Association are Research and Development in automotive engineering for industry, product design and development, evaluation of automotive equipment and ancillaries, standardisation, technical information services, execution of advanced courses on the application of modern technology and conduct of specific tests.

  • CIRT

    The Central Institute of Road Transport (CIRT[2]) is a Government of India undertaking, established under the then Ministry of Shipping and Transport and the Association of State Road Transport Undertakings (ASTRU). It is a nodal agency, known to be only one of its kind,[3] providing a platform for higher research and training on road transportation and assists in testing, guidance and consultancy in the field.

  • GARC

    Global Automotive Research Centre (GARC) is one of the state of art test centre established by Ministry of Heavy Industries and PE, Govt. of India (under NATRIP), located at Oragadam near Chennai, Tamilnadu. GARC has been authorized as test agency under CMVR 126 by Ministry of Road Transport & Highways, Govt. of India. GARC has the full-fledged R&D and Homologation Test Facilities including the Test Tracks to certify all category of vehicles, systems and components as per national and international standards.

  • NATRAX

    NATRAX is one of the state-of-the-art automotive testing and certification centre under NATRiP, a flagship project of the Ministry of Heavy Industries, Govt of India planned under the Automotive Mission Plan launched by Government of India. It is located near the vibrant industrial township of Pithampur. (Dist.   • NATRIP

    National Automotive Testing and R&D Infrastructure Project (NATRiP), the largest and one of the most significant initiatives in automotive sector so far, represents a unique joining of hands between the Government of India, a number of state governments and Indian Automotive Industry to create a state of the art Testing, Validation and R&D infrastructure in the country.

  • VRDE

    Vehicle Research and Development Establishment (VRDE) is a laboratory of the Defence Research & Development Organization (DRDO) located at Vahannagar near Ahmednagar. Its primary function is research and development of various light tracked, wheeled and specialised vehicles for defence applications.

  • NIAIMT

    NIAIMT, Silchar center is implemented under National Automotive Testing and R&D Infrastructure Project (NATRiP), the largest and one of the most significant initiatives in Automotive sector so far by Govt. of India. NIAIMT is one of its kind center in India as well as in North East. NIAIMT has two campuses.
